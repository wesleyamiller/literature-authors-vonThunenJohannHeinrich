# Chronological Overview of the Writings of Johann Heinrich von Thuenen

**Johann Heinrich von Thuenen** (1783--1850) was a Mecklenburg landowner, agricultural economist, and proto-marginalist thinker. From his estate at Tellow, southeast of Rostock, he spent decades collecting data on crop yields, labor costs, and transport expenses, synthesizing them into the theoretical framework of *Der isolirte Staat* -- one of the foundational works of spatial economics and location theory.

This corpus contains 37 identified works spanning 1803 to 1863. Of these, 33 have machine-readable text (89% coverage). The works fall into four categories: the multi-part *Isolated State* treatise, periodical articles for the Mecklenburg Agricultural Society, conference papers, and occasional writings.

---

## Early Career (1803--1818)

Thuenen trained at Lucas Staudinger's agricultural institute in Gross Flottbeck (near Hamburg) under the patronage of Baron Caspar Voght, then farmed in several locations before purchasing the Tellow estate in 1810. His earliest publications are practical reports on local agriculture.

## Tellow Experiments (1819--1832)

## Late Works and the Completion of *Der isolirte Staat* (1841--1863)

In 1841 Thuenen presented at the Doberan agricultural congress. In 1842 he published a revised second edition of Part 1. He spent his final years working on Part 2, which introduced the "natural wage" formula (sqrt(ap)) -- a result he valued so highly he asked for it to be engraved on his tombstone. He died in 1850 with the work unfinished; the remaining parts were published posthumously.

## Thematic Threads

Thuenen's 37 works weave together several strands:

**Location and rent theory.** The *Isolated State* (W001--W004) is the core. But the practical foundation appears throughout: transport-cost calculations (W017), wool-market location analysis (W026), and the constant comparison of returns at different distances from market.

**Agricultural experimentation.** Thuenen was among the first to apply quantitative methods to farming. His 20-year unfertilized plot (W036), fertilizer studies (W007, W016, W022), tillage experiments (W027, W031, W032), and feed-value analysis (W013, W037) represent a proto-scientific approach to agriculture decades before formal agricultural experiment stations.

**Institutional economics.** Credit systems (W006), banks (W008), agricultural institutes (W018), wool-market infrastructure (W025, W026), and tax reform (W034) show Thuenen thinking about the institutional conditions for economic development.

**The natural wage.** The mathematical relationship sqrt(ap) -- connecting subsistence (a) and productivity (p) -- appears in Part 2 (W002, W003) and the 1848 fragment (W010). It was the culmination of Thuenen's lifelong effort to derive economic laws from first principles and empirical data.
