XI

Continuation of the Experiment on Deepening the Topsoil at the Schwandt Estate.

Cf. Annalen, 26th volume, pp. 665 and 668.

Count von Schlieffen of Schwandt has continued this experiment further than the plan of the task set by the Patriotic Society called for, and has been kind enough to share with me the results of the experiment from the year 1843.

The experiment can certainly yield all the more insights the longer it is continued, and with the permission of the Count I hereby present his communication to me to the public in what follows.

Each field of 2/4 square rods in size was sown on October 1, 1842, with 2 pounds of rye.

In grain and straw combined, the fields rank as follows: 1, 2, 6, 4, 5, 3, 7, 8.

Grain alone: 1, 6, 2, 5, 7, 4, 3, 8.

Straw alone: 1, 2, 4, 5, 6, 3, 7, 8.

In the result of this harvest there is again more consistency, although field No. 3, as with the peas, stands very unfavorably and No. 6 very favorably — but the stand of the preceding crop is likely the main cause of this.

The rye in the large field was consistently better throughout, and is to be estimated at the 6th grain. J. H. von Thunen.
